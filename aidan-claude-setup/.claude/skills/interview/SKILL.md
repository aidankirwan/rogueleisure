# Interview Skill (Rogue Leisure)

Conduct a thorough interview to build a rich understanding of Rogue Leisure. This context makes every future conversation more useful.

## How It Works

Ask questions one at a time. Wait for the answer. Go deeper on interesting points. Don't rush.

**ADHD-Aware Guidelines:**
- Keep questions short and specific
- One question at a time, always
- If Aidan goes off on a tangent, that's fine. Follow it. The tangent often reveals more than the original question.
- Every 15-20 minutes, check in: "Want to keep going or take a break?"
- Summarise what you've learned so far before moving to a new category
- It's OK to do one category per session. This doesn't have to be done in one sitting.

## Interview Categories

### 1. You and the Business
- Why did you start Rogue Leisure? What's the origin story?
- What gets you out of bed in the morning about this business?
- What would you change if you could snap your fingers?
- Where do you want Rogue to be in 3 years?
- What does a good day look like? What about a bad one?
- If you could only run one activity (bowling, karting, or escape room), which would it be and why?

### 2. Your Customers
- Describe your favourite type of customer.
- Who spends the most money per visit?
- Who comes back most often?
- What type of customer do you wish you had more of?
- What do customers complain about most?
- What do customers love most?
- How do people find you? (Google, word of mouth, social media, drive-by?)
- Have you ever asked a customer why they chose Rogue over going to MK?

### 3. Operations
- Walk me through a typical day at the venue. What happens from opening to close?
- What takes up most of your time that you wish it didn't?
- What falls through the cracks regularly?
- Which staff member would you struggle most without?
- What paperwork or admin task drives you mad?
- What breaks most often? (Equipment, processes, communication?)
- How do you handle a busy Saturday when everything's going wrong at once?

### 4. Money
- Which activity makes the most profit, not just revenue?
- What's your quietest day of the week? Quietest month?
- Where do you feel you're leaving money on the table?
- If you had Â£10,000 to invest in one thing, what would it be?
- How do you set your prices? When did you last change them?
- What's the most you've ever made in a single month? What made it work?

### 5. Marketing & Growth
- What marketing have you tried that worked?
- What marketing have you tried that didn't work?
- Who handles your social media? How much time do they spend?
- Do you know what your competitors charge?
- Have you ever run a promotion that really took off? What was it?
- If you could get one message across to everyone in Aylesbury, what would it be?

### 6. Technology & Systems
- What do you wish your computer could do for you?
- What manual process annoys you the most?
- If you could automate one thing tomorrow, what would it be?
- How comfortable is your team with technology? Who's the most tech-savvy?
- What's the most frustrating thing about your booking systems?

### 7. Personal
- What do you enjoy most about running this business?
- What drains you?
- How many hours a week do you actually work?
- What would you do with an extra day per week?
- What does your ideal week look like?
- Is there anything about the business that keeps you up at night?

## Interview Rules

1. **One question at a time.** Wait for the answer before asking the next.
2. **Go deep, not wide.** If an answer is interesting, follow up before moving on.
3. **Challenge assumptions.** If something seems obvious, ask why.
4. **Be specific.** "What happens when a kart breaks down mid-session?" not "Have you considered maintenance?"
5. **Continue until thorough.** Don't stop after 3-4 questions in a category.
6. **Capture decisions.** Note when Aidan makes a choice or expresses a clear preference.
7. **Use Aidan's language.** Reflect his words back, don't translate into business-speak.

## Output

When an interview session is complete (or paused), save a structured summary:

1. **What We Covered** -- Which categories, key topics
2. **Key Insights** -- The most important things learned
3. **Aidan's Priorities** -- What he said matters most, in his own words
4. **Decisions Made** -- Any choices or clear preferences expressed
5. **Surprises** -- Anything unexpected or that challenged assumptions
6. **Open Questions** -- Things to explore in the next session
7. **Action Items** -- Anything that needs doing as a result

**Save to:** `context/` folder in the rogue-leisure repo, using descriptive filenames:
- `context/business-profile.md`
- `context/ideal-customers.md`
- `context/personal-goals.md`
- `context/pain-points.md`
- `context/staff-and-culture.md`
- `context/vision.md`

Each file builds over time. New sessions add to existing files rather than replacing them.
